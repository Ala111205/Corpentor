CORPENTRY

ABOUT

Carpentry is a skilled trade and craft that involves cutting, shaping, and installing building materials during the construction of buildings, ships, timber bridges, concrete formwork, etc. Carpenters traditionally worked with natural wood and did rougher work such as framing, but today many other materials are also used and sometimes the finer trades of cabinetmaking and furniture building are considered carpentry. Carpenters are highly trained in their craft and possess a deep understanding of woodworking techniques and principles.

Carpenter Workbench

In carpentry, a bench refers to a flat, smooth surface used for specific tasks. It provides a stable work area for activities such as engineering design work, machining, tooling, woodworking, metalwork, and project design.

Furniture Carpenter

The furniture carpenter job description generally entails designing, building, and repairing various types of furniture such as wooden chairs, shelves, and drawers. They are skilled in the construction of wooden fixtures commonly found in homes/offices.

Construction Carpenter

The construction carpenter job description entails reading blueprints, drawings, and sketches to have a clear understanding of the requirements. Measuring and calculating the quantity and cost of materials required to complete the task are also duties that they perform.

Finishing Corpenter

Finish carpentry refers to the final touches that make a house a home. Finish carpenters work on homes after the structure is fully built, insulated, wired, and plumbed.
